How-To Guides

Learn the basics of fire management, smoke control, and food safety so every cook feels more relaxed and predictable.

How to Set Up Your Smoker

  1. Clean out old ash so air can flow freely.
  2. Fill a charcoal basket or arrange coals for indirect heat.
  3. Add a few wood chunks on top of the charcoal (not soaked).
  4. Open intake and exhaust vents fully to start.
  5. Once near target temp, slowly close the intake vent to stabilize.

Goal: Stable temperature with thin, blue smoke (not thick white clouds).

How to Use a Meat Thermometer

  1. Insert the probe into the thickest part of the meat, avoiding bones.
  2. For larger cuts (pork shoulder, roast), check in multiple spots.
  3. Use a leave-in probe for long cooks and an instant-read for final checks.
  4. Don’t chase exact numbers — look for a range and tenderness.
  5. Let meat rest after hitting target temperature before slicing.

Tip: Temperature is a guide; tenderness and feel matter just as much.

Choosing Wood & Smoke Level

  • Fruit woods (apple, cherry): Mild, great for chicken and pork.
  • Oak: Medium smoke, very versatile.
  • Hickory / Mesquite: Strong smoke, easy to overdo for beginners.

Start with less wood than you think you need. You can always add more, but you can’t take smoke away.

Managing a Long Cook Without Stress

  1. Pre-heat the smoker for 30–45 minutes before adding food.
  2. Log times and temperatures in a notebook or notes app.
  3. Resist opening the lid unless you need to wrap, spritz, or check probes.
  4. Add fuel in small amounts to avoid big temp swings.
  5. Plan your cook so meat finishes early and can rest in a warm cooler.

Mindset: Small adjustments, slow changes — let the smoker do the work.

Food Safety Reminder

Always refrigerate meat promptly, avoid cross-contamination between raw and cooked foods, and follow USDA guidelines for minimum internal temperatures and safe handling.
