Starter Recipes

These recipes are built for beginners using real backyard gear. Focus on temps, time, and feel — not fancy ingredients.

Beginner Pulled Pork (Pork Shoulder)

One of the most forgiving cooks. Perfect for your first long smoke.

Best for: Parties, tacos, sliders

Cook time: 8–12 hours

Target temp: 195–203°F internal

  • Rub: equal parts salt, pepper, garlic, plus a little paprika
  • Smoker temp: 250°F
  • Wrap in foil or butcher paper around 160–165°F if it’s stalling

Smoked Chicken Thighs

Quick, juicy, and super forgiving. Great weeknight smoke.

Best for: Meal prep, BBQ plates

Cook time: 1.5–2 hours

Target temp: 175–185°F internal (for extra tender)

  • Dry brine with salt in the fridge for 2–12 hours
  • Smoker temp: 275°F
  • Brush on your favorite BBQ sauce in the last 10–15 minutes

Simple Backyard Ribs

Classic backyard ribs with a light smoke flavor and gentle bite.

Best for: Weekends, family BBQ

Cook time: ~5–6 hours

Target temp: 195°F internal or bend test

  • Trim membrane from the back of the rack
  • Smoker temp: 250°F
  • Optional: follow a loose 2–2–1 style (smoke, wrapped, unwrapped)

Smoked Chuck Roast (“Poor Man’s Brisket”)

All the brisket vibes with cheaper meat and a slightly shorter cook.

Best for: Sliced plates or chopped sandwiches

Cook time: 6–8 hours

Target temp: 200–205°F internal

  • Rub: salt, pepper, garlic, onion powder
  • Smoker temp: 250–265°F
  • Rest for at least 45–60 minutes before slicing

Quick Internal Temperature Guide

Meat Finished Internal Temp Notes
Pork Shoulder 195–203°F Shred when the probe slides in “like butter.”
Chicken Thighs 175–185°F Dark meat stays juicy even at higher temps.
Pork Ribs ~195°F Use the bend test or toothpick test to confirm tenderness.
Beef Chuck Roast 200–205°F Rest in a cooler or wrapped for best texture.

Always use a reliable instant-read thermometer and follow food safety guidelines.
